The Space Suit

I am Character. Vagabond mathematician, travel writer. Perhaps you've read my work? Likely not, for fame is not mine. But it will be --- ahem --- might be. That is to say, I'm in a bit of a jam. Having recently become helpless prey. The victim of a creature somewhat akin to your world's Ammophila sabulosa. The displeasure of our meeting resulted in my paralysis and subsequent storage in a subterranean pantry. Thus this message is a plea to you, kind reader, wherever you may be.

I'm in a cave, in a flat dimension unlike your own. Beside me is a horrible larva which will soon awaken and devour me. In a desperate bid to escape I've hastily penned a piece of software. Said software is installed on your world's Web. Said software is a crude implementation of a trans-dimensional Application. Time did not permit the polish you may be accustomed too, and I apologize. The Application couples your Web with the root system of a remarkable tree. A tree which grows across dimensions. One of the roots has penetrated this pantry prison, and through it I hope to escape.

The Application contains my spare Space Suit, which will enable the wearer to enter a plane of your dimension. Please, bring this suit to me. I am in dire need of rescue.

Important technical information: Aside the printed markers, this prototype requires a very modern browser. You'll need either Mozilla Firefox Aurora or Google Chrome Canary. If you are using Aurora, open about:config and set media.navigator.enabled to true. On startup the browser will ask for your authorization before the webcam is activated. Both Aurora and Canary are as unpredicable/unstable as advertised, so if you experience any problems then a page reload might be the only solution.

This prototype employs a number of wonderful Open Source projects. They are:

  • JSAR Toolkit: https://github.com/kig/JSARToolKit, a javascript port of FLAR Toolkit, which is Flash port of ARToolkit
  • THREE.JS: https://github.com/mrdoob/three.js/
  • RequireJS: http://requirejs.org/
